The Evolution of The Kanye Rant

Grammy award winning rapper Kanye West is no stranger to pushing buttons. From the first time he came on the scene, he's never held his tongue. Many people, however, may look at his outbursts as random, rude or they just completely misunderstand his message. His rants, however, are directly parallel to the lyrics in his music. In this timeline, the top bar represents 7 of his rants and the bottom bar represents 7 of his song lyrics that coorelate to what he said. Though this is only a snapshot of West's many rants, this highlights some of the most remembered.

Sept. 6, 2005: When West went off script during a benefit concert for Hurricane Katrina live on NBC, even co-host Mike Myers raised an eyebrow. He declared George Bush's apathy towards black people. George Bush said his comment was "disgusting" and West later apologized for his remark.

West's sophomore album, late registration, shows Hurrican Katrina isn't where his skepticism with Bush began, insinuating that the government is the driving force behind crack in black communities: "Who gave Saddam anthrax?/George Bush got the answers/Back in the hood it's a different type of chemical/Arm and Hammer baking soda raised they own quota."

Oct. 9, 2006: When Justice and Simian's "We Are Your Friends" video beat West's "Touch the Sky" at the MTV Europe Music Awards, West crashed the stage arguing that his video was the best.

West makes it clear that he doesn't bite his tongue, especially on the testosterone filled "You Can't Tell Me Nothing. He raps: "I'm on TV talking like it's just you and me/I'm just saying how I feel man".

June 13, 2008: West was two hours late for his performance at Bonnaroo and fans were pissed, following musician Robert Randolph in chanting "Kanye sucks!" the next day at his set. Ye caught wind of it and responded on his blog.

In "Runaway," West admits his flaws: "And I always find, yeah, I always find something wrong."

Sept. 13, 2009: Probably one of his most memorable interruptions, West intercepted the microphone when Taylor Swift was awarded Video of the Year at the 2009 VMA's. He argued that Beyonce should have won with a simple statement and exited with a shrug as if to say, "I'm just stating facts."

In "Runaway," West admits his flaws: "And I always find, yeah, I always find something wrong."
